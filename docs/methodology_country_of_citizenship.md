# Methodology Note: Country Of Citizenship

`country of citizenship` (`P27`) is useful evidence, but it is weak as a
single historical nationality category for eighteenth-century writers.

The field tends to work best for modern nation-state biographies. It is less
stable for:

- empires and dynastic states;
- colonies and imperial subjects;
- people born before modern citizenship categories;
- successor states whose present-day labels do not match eighteenth-century
  political geography;
- mobile writers whose birth, death, residence, work location, and writing
  language point to different affiliations.

For this project, `P27` should be treated as one signal in an evidence matrix,
not as the final nationality assignment.

The recommended minimum evidence families are:

```text
country of citizenship
birth-place context
death-place context
residence
work location
spoken/written language
writing language
native language
```

This distinction is especially important for China/Qing and British imperial
contexts. In the legacy French seed, exact citizenship does not recover either
context well. In the global writer cohort, exact citizenship recovers many
Chinese/Qing cases but still barely captures British imperial context; place
evidence recovers many more British imperial cases.

Therefore, conference claims should distinguish:

```text
citizenship representation
geographic/place-context representation
language representation
derived cultural or national affiliation
```

Keeping those categories separate is not a limitation of the method. It is one
of the method's findings: the chosen data category changes which historical
people become visible.
